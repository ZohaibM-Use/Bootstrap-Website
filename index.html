Earth

The Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body that supports life. It is home to a diverse range of ecosystems which provide the necessary resources for humans, animals, and plants to thrive. Over the course of Earth's history, the landscape has changed many times.

Moon

The Moon is a natural satellite of the Earth, composed primarily of rock and metal. It orbits our planet at an average distance of about 384,400 kilometers, completing a full orbit roughly every 27.3 days. The Moon plays a crucial role in Earth's natural processes, influencing tides due to its gravitational pull and stabilizing the planet's axial tilt, which helps maintain a relatively stable climate.
